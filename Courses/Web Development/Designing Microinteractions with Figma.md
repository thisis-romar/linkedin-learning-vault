>

> **[0:03](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=3)** Microinteractions are tiny interaction moments that occur at a given time in the user interface.
>
> **[0:09](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=9)** And overall, every micro interaction uses a trigger feedback mechanism that consists of four simple steps and those steps are trigger, rules, feedback, and finally, loops and modes.
>
> **[0:26](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=26)** Trigger is the first step that initiates microinteraction.

> **[0:30](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=30)** Triggers can be initiated either by a user or even by a system itself.
>
> **[0:35](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=35)** A common example of a trigger that is initiated by a user is when you click, for example on a button or hover over it with your mouths.

> **[0:45](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=45)** In contrast, when a system system initiates a trigger it involves a set of rules that have to be met in order for microinteraction to appear.
>
> **[0:54](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=54)** So for example, a warning or a text notification that you see on your screen.
>
> **[1:02](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=62)** The next step of microinteractions are rules and they define exactly what happens once the microinteraction is triggered.
>
> **[1:10](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=70)** A very good example of a rule is a light switch on your phone.

> **[1:14](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=74)** So after you triggered a light switch with your button then the light turns on and the rule is that this light is always on unless you switch it off.
>
> **[1:25](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=85)** Now we have feedback which lets the users know that something has happened after microinteraction has been triggered.
>
> **[1:32](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=92)** And very often feedback contains visual aspects such as color change, tool tap appearance, or text notification.
>
> **[1:39](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=99)** For example, when you interact with a web shop and click on the purchase or complete purchase button then you either see a confirmation or an error message that explicitly tells you that your payment has been completed or not.
>
> **[1:52](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=112)** And this is in a sort of way is a good feedback that you as a user receive.
>
> **[1:58](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=118)** Finally, we have loops and modes that define how microinteraction changes over time when used repeatedly, and going back to the web shop example the add to card button changes immediately to the checkout button after you clicked on it because the conditions changed and this is the change that we get in our microinteraction as well.
>
> **[2:19](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=139)** And now let's quickly talk why microinteractions are so important.
>
> **[2:24](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=144)** Well, microinteractions benefit both users and businesses as they attract new users and increase user retention.
>
> **[2:33](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=153)** Microinteractions also provide users with context and let them know that something is currently happening.
>
> **[2:40](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=160)** With real-time feedback, microinteractions also prevent users from making errors which is actually leading to a better user experience.
>
> **[2:48](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-microinteractions-with-figma/about-microinteractions?u=76281980&t=168)** And overall microinteractions give the user a feeling of control, increased engagement, and make the whole experience more joyful and pleasant.
