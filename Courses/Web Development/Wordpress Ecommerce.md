#### [PCI compliance](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/wordpress-ecommerce-2/pci-compliance?u=76281980)

> [!transcript]- Transcript
>
> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/wordpress-ecommerce-2/pci-compliance?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Instructor] One of the scariest parts of eCommerce is handling sensitive information like credit card numbers. And they're so easy to be misused by a malicious entity, they're heavily-controlled by the [payment card industry data security standard](../../Glossary/Standard/PCI%20DSS.md), aka, PCIDSS, which is usually called PCI compliance. The idea of PCI compliance is to keep all consumer credit numbers secure so there are limitations on what a business can do. Generally speaking, they can't store numbers in plain text. You have to transmit those numbers securely, and you have to limit how many people in the organization can access those numbers. For big businesses that actually store credit card numbers, there is a lot of regulation. Luckily, for most eCommerce companies, you aren't actually storing credit card information. The website sends the checkout page to your browser, which you fill in. The checkout fields go back to the website. But the credit card information is usually sent directly to the payment gateway, which then alerts your website. That means the credit card numbers don't touch your website. And even if your website remembers credit card numbers, there's actually clever technology in the background called credit card tokenization, that stores a special token that only your website can use. You spend a little bit more money on R&D, branding, and [Product Development](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Product%20Development.md), but you spend far less in acquisition costs over the course of the product's life cycle. If you have one of these [products](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Microsoft%20Products.md), great. However, the vast majority of products need a little bit of helping getting people to come back. There are three really good ways to remind people that you exist and they should come back to you. Social media, [Newsletters](../../Skills/Web%20Development/Newsletters.md), and marketing automation. Encouraging customers to follow you on social media is a good way to maintain contact with your customers. Every time a customer sees one of your posts, it's a subtle reminder that you still exist and that they should come back and visit your store. You can't just blast sales all the time. You have to give people a reason to follow you. That usually means giving them helpful tips and resources and occasionally sending one of your own promotions. You should always give users the option to join your newsletter when they checkout. It's only one extra check box. Newsletters let you segment your audience. That means you can send specific e-mails to specific people. So you could send an e-mail to everyone who bought your first product that a new version is coming out and to give them a special discount. This way you aren't giving everyone the discount, and your existing customers also feel rewarded.
>
> **[1:34](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/wordpress-ecommerce-2/increasing-clv-with-repeat-customers?u=76281980&t=94)** Getting someone's e-mail address is one of the most direct methods of contact. Social networks are great, but at some point those social networks are going to lose popularity and you'll have to rebuild your brand on a new site, whereas people tend to keep the same e-mail for a long time. Taking newsletter marketing to the next level can be done with marketing automation. Using purchase or browsing data, you can automatically send customers targeted e-mails, also called auto responders. With marketing automation, you can sell the customer on a related item. If a user bought a blueberry protein bar you could send an auto responder to let them know that you also offer blueberry vitamin water. You can remind customers of your brand by sending them an e-mail on their birthday, or, if they haven't made a purchase in awhile, enticing them to come back with a coupon. You can even build a sequence of steeply discounted coupons to engage those customers. These are called discount ladders. There's a great service called Jilt that helps users with abandoned carts as well as marketing automation. They have a freemium plan, so the initial costs to you are zero. If you want to explore e-mail automation this is a great place to start. Reminding people to come back to your site is much easier than finding new customers. You should think about ways to get people to reengage with you after each purchase.
