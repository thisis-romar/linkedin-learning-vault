> [!transcript]- Transcript
>
> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/introducing-robotic-process-automation-18500442/a-simplified-definition-of-rpa?u=76281980&t=0)** - The definition presented in the last lesson was comprehensive, but was also a bit technical. So I'd like to introduce a real world analogy that often helps me explain RPA to enterprises just getting started. Imagine you've just hired someone new to your team. In this case, like bonus payments or final paychecks for anyone leaving the company or other more esoteric transactions. Eventually your new hire will have learned their job and will be fully self-sufficient, and hopefully they remember how to do each process as you've taught them. Think of these as your field-tested guardrails for building automations that last. One, engage IT early, especially when AI is in the mix. You need them for access and infrastructure readiness. Two, document ruthlessly, full, step-by-step processes, clear assumptions, metrics. Use process intelligence tools, but validate with human expertise. Three, govern for both RPA and AI.
