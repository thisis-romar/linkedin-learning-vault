Asynchronous work, on the other hand, is about empowering you, the worker, to do great work without that distraction. One of the biggest dangers of remote working is simply replicating what we already know from the office. Everyone understands how to operate in the office, and a lot of things just happen naturally, without anything really being prompted or expected. The difference with asynchronous work is that it's not as visible. Managers can't see workers doing work, and their requirement or desire often falls into wanting to micromanage people, and really destroying that ability to do deep, focused work. The responsibility of the organization, then, is to take a step back, to trust their people, and allow great remote work to happen. The easiest way to do that is to empower them with an understanding that asynchronous work is acceptable. Asynchronous work allows them to do it.
