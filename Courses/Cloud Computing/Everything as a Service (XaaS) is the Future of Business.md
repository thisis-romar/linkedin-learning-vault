This combination of internet standard technology, broadband networks, and low costs enabled more technology to be delivered remotely. This is the first time it became viable to have complex applications delivered from a distant location. For organizations that wanted to outsource some of their computing applications in order to reduce costs and focus on their core competencies instead, they could consume computing solutions from an application service provider or ASP. An ASP would build or host an existing application, provide all the software maintenance and make it available for a business for a monthly or annual fee. The business user would access the application from their networked computer. The ASP typically hosted a full version of the application for each buyer and so their model was typically limited by their own infrastructure. This constraint is one of the reasons the ASP model wasn't sustainable in the longterm. That said, because of its clear business benefits, the ASP model became popular for several years. The ASP option for organizations
>
> **[1:38](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/everything-as-a-service-xaas-is-the-future-of-business/a-brief-history-of-xaas?u=76281980&t=98)** hasn't completely disappeared, but it's largely being displaced by its successor, [software as a service](../../Glossary/Concept/SaaS.md) or [SaaS](../../Glossary/Concept/SaaS.md). Why did this happen? SaaS offers the benefits of ASPs such as outsourcing the hosting and maintenance of applications, a subscription-based model versus capital cost, remote access, system security, speed to deploy and lower costs. But SaaS has several additional benefits over ASPs. While ASPs generally mimic an organization's data center services by hosting application needs, including software from many vendors, a SaaS solution is generally a full service, web-enabled solution that is developed exclusively by the SaaS provider. The customer uses the SaaS solution from any device with an internet, browser and connection. Any special onsite requirements for the customer are eliminated. That's why today you can access most SaaS software as easily from your business laptop connected to the corporate network as your smartphone used from a beach on your vacation. The ability to extend access to a multitude of rich computing solutions for both providers and customers from any device, at any time from anywhere at relatively low cost is a central breakthrough

The SaaS model of as a service, delivered over the internet quickly expanded to include infrastructures as a service, [IaaS](../../Glossary/Concept/IaaS.md), that includes services such as storage and data backups and [platform as a service](../../Glossary/Concept/PaaS.md), [PaaS](../../Glossary/Concept/PaaS.md), which includes [Databases](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Databases.md) and programming environments. Many more as a services are coming online. Together, we now refer to these services as everything as a service or X as a service, and this capability is bringing completely new value to businesses.
