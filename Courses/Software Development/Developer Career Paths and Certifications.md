It's important to think of your work life as a journey instead of a single sprint. Developing a career is more than landing that first or even second job. It involves thinking about where you want to be in the future. Now, one of the reasons having a plan is so important is so that managers or mentors can help getting you there. Good mentors will ask you about your goals, but you can only talk about the goals that you have. Make a list of the things you want to learn and the sort of projects you'd like to work on. Interested in becoming a manager? Developers are generally satisfied with their jobs, so not all of them feel the need to become a manager, although they usually make more money. You should become a manager if you enjoy building teams and mentoring more or as much as you like programming. Some developers can enjoy leading teams or projects without getting into management. Now think about all these things and write down a one-year and a five-year plan of where you'd like to be. Take a look at it at least once a year before your performance reviews. Plans are flexible, but it's important to write them down so that you and those around you can work on getting you there.
