> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-business/three-levels-of-biodiversity-23769341?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Biodiversity](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Biodiversity.md), or the variety of life on Earth, operates on three distinct levels: genetic diversity, species diversity, and ecosystem diversity. Let's look into each level and the significance they hold for our planet and business. Number one: genetic diversity. At the core of biodiversity is genetic diversity, the variety of genes within a species. This diversity ensures resilience to environmental changes, diseases, and other challenges. According to a study published in the journal "Nature," genetic diversity is a key factor in the adaptability of species to environmental changes. For example, maintaining diverse genetic resources is vital for producing crops that can withstand [Climate Change](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Climate%20Change.md), pests, and diseases. This is a key contributor to global food security and feeding a planet with 10 billion people by 2050. Number two: species diversity. Earth is home to an estimated 8.7 million species. Species diversity refers to the variety of different species coexisting in an ecosystem. Each species plays a unique role in maintaining the balance of our ecosystems, from pollination of plants, to the decomposition of organic matter that creates soils and minerals. Businesses involved in agriculture, fisheries, pharmaceuticals, and many others rely on the biodiversity of species for resources and innovation.
>
> **[1:38](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-business/three-levels-of-biodiversity-23769341?u=76281980&t=98)** The loss of species diversity poses massive challenges for business, such as potential disruptions to supply chains and the diminishing availability of essential raw materials in general. Number three: ecosystem diversity. Ecosystem diversity encompasses the variety of ecosystems on Earth, from forests and wetlands, to deserts and oceans. A study published in the journal "Nature Communications" highlights that ecosystems with higher diversity are more productive and resilient, providing essential services like clean water, air purification, climate regulation, and many more benefits. For businesses, ecosystem diversity is crucial for [Sustainability](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Sustainability.md). The degradation of ecosystems leads to increased costs, regulatory challenges, and reputational risks. Industries dependent on natural resources, and that's pretty much everyone, must recognize the interconnectedness between their operations and the health of Earth's ecosystems. The challenges businesses face in preserving biodiversity are clear. Climate change, habitat destruction, and overexploitation of resources are ongoing threats. As scary as this can all sound, companies can become a leading source for solving these problems by investing in sustainable practices, supporting conservation initiatives, and integrating biodiversity considerations throughout their business strategies.

#### [Five pressures driving biodiversity loss](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-business/five-pressures-driving-biodiversity-loss-23763475?u=76281980)

> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-business/five-pressures-driving-biodiversity-loss-23763475?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Biodiversity](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Biodiversity.md) on Earth is under tremendous threat from various pressures, and understanding these pressures is crucial to developing effective strategies for conservation. Let's explore five major drivers of biodiversity loss and their implications for business. They are habitat destruction, [Climate Change](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Climate%20Change.md), over exploitation, pollution, and invasive species. One of the primary drivers of biodiversity loss is habitat destruction. The conversion of natural habitats into urban areas, agricultural lands and infrastructure has profound consequences. Much of the world's land-based biodiversity is found in forests and deforestation is a significant contributor to species loss, as is the loss of native grasslands and other land-based natural habitat. Life under water, like in our oceans, lakes, and rivers, faces similar challenges. Climate change is significantly impacting biodiversity. Rising temperatures, extreme weather events and shifting precipitation patterns, disrupt ecosystems and affect the distribution of species. The United Nations predicts that climate change will become one of the major drivers of biodiversity loss in the coming decades, and we're beginning to see that happen already. Businesses are increasingly recognizing the risks associated with climate change and biodiversity loss from supply chain vulnerabilities to increased operational costs.
>
> **[1:36](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-business/five-pressures-driving-biodiversity-loss-23763475?u=76281980&t=96)** Mitigating these risks requires a shift towards sustainable practices and the reduction of carbon and nature footprints. Over exploitation refers to the excessive and unsustainable use of natural resources and including overfishing mining and forest destruction. The UN Convention on Biological Diversity estimates that over 30% of the world's fish stocks are over exploited. This kind of pressure poses a direct threat to species and ecosystems, affecting both marine and land-based environments. Not to mention the people depended on them. For businesses, over exploitation means resource scarcity, regulatory restrictions, and license to operate limitations. Adopting sustainable resource management practices is essential for long-term business resilience and profitability. Pollution, including air, water, and soil pollution, poses a significant threat to biodiversity. The World Health Organization reports that pollution is responsible for an estimated 9 million premature deaths annually. Chemical pollutants harm ecosystems and contribute to the decline of various species. Just think of the recent news on forever chemicals, leaching into drinking water and the microplastics we're now finding in the food we eat and beverages we drink. For businesses, pollution generating practices can lead to legal issues and massive fines,
>
> **[3:10](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-business/five-pressures-driving-biodiversity-loss-23763475?u=76281980&t=190)** remediation mandates, increased operational costs for waste management and serious damage to brand reputation. 3M, DuPont, Volkswagen and other brand name companies are just three well-known examples of companies whose brand equity has taken severe hits from pollution based issues, and there are many others. The introduction of invasive species into new ecosystems can outcompete native species, disrupt food webs and lead to population declines. Invasive species are a significant driver of biodiversity loss in forested areas on islands and in fresh and saltwater ecosystems. For example, over the last 20 years, massive outbreaks of mountain pine beetles in western North America have killed millions of acres of forest from New Mexico to British Columbia. Businesses should help prevent the spread of invasive species, especially around ocean and lakes based cargo and freight transportation. Recognizing the interconnectedness between biodiversity loss and business [Sustainability](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Sustainability.md) is key. By taking proactive measures to protect biodiversity, companies can create a world where both they and biodiversity mutually thrive.
