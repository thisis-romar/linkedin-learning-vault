So let's talk about the different kinds of supply chain credentials you can earn and the types of organizations that award them. A credential is any document that you receive to verify you've attained a skill. There are three main types of credentials; certificates, certifications, and degrees. Certificates are usually the easiest to get. You sometimes earn a certificate just for completing a class or taking an exam. In fact, you'll earn a certificate from [LinkedIn](../../Skills/Web%20Development/LinkedIn.md) learning when you complete this course. Certifications are usually a bit harder. For certifications, you may be required to meet some prerequisite requirements, like holding a degree or having on-the-job experience. There's almost always a test for a certification and there are often continuing education requirements to keep your certification current. Degrees or diplomas are usually the most difficult and the most expensive to earn. They're typically granted by universities, and they often need to comply with accreditation requirements. But once you've earned a degree, you hold that title forever. Depending on your goals, you'll probably need to earn a combination of certificates, certifications, and degrees as you navigate your supply chain career.

When it comes to supply chain certifications, most of the popular ones are offered by or in cooperation with not-for-profit supply chain associations, but there are some certifications that are issued by for-profit companies.
