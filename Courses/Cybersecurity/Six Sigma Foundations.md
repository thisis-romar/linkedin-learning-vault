>
> **[0:34](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=34)** As customers, our immediate questions are why does it take so long and why so much uncertainty as to when the technician shows up?

> **[0:44](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=44)** With that example as our backdrop, let's explore the five phases.

>
> **[0:58](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=58)** The team is tasked with understanding what is important to customers and determine the performance outcome to be improved.
>
> **[1:05](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=65)** In Six Sigma, the performance outcome to be improved is known as the Y, as in Y is the function of x.

> **[1:23](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=83)** In the measure phase, the size and scope of the problem is understood and performance on Y is measured.

> **[1:57](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=117)** In total, it takes five to 10 days before a technician can show up at your door.

> **[2:03](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=123)** Due to this variation, the call center has a schedule 10 days out.

>
> **[2:16](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=136)** The data is analyzed to determine the causes of factors that impact performance.
>
> **[2:21](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=141)** In other words, we diagnose and prove which X factors impact the Y.

> **[2:26](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=146)** Back to our cable company.
>
> **[2:28](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=148)** Why does it take so long, and why is there so much variation at each step?

>
> **[2:39](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=159)** The X factors that drive performance Y are determined.
>
> **[2:43](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=163)** Then, we move on to improve.
>
> **[2:46](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=166)** In improve phase, solutions are developed to address the proven X factors so that Y can be improved.
>
> **[2:53](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=173)** Solutions are developed, piloted, and implemented to reduce the length and the variability of processing times.
>
> **[3:02](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=182)** In our example, this results in the whole process, from call center to engineering to dispatch and the field technician showing up, taking less than 24 hours.

> **[3:13](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=193)** Finally, we move on to control.
>
> **[3:16](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=196)** In the control phase, controls are established to ensure that our improvements, or gains, are sustainable.
>
> **[3:24](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=204)** Controls and procedures are put in place so that employees know when and how to intervene to ensure superior performance.
>
> **[3:34](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=214)** In our example, the total time is reduced from 10 days down to one day, and the unpredictability of when the technician shows up is reduced from an eight hour window down to a one hour window.
>
> **[3:48](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=228)** By determining the key X factors that impact Y, we can be proactive in ensuring a superior Y performance simply by controlling the key X factors.
>
> **[3:59](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/an-overview-of-dmaic-2?u=76281980&t=239)** The result is that we are now better able to plan, predict, and deliver as promised.

> **[0:44](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=44)** That is why the control plan is so important during the control phase of a Six Sigma project.
>
> **[0:50](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=50)** The control plan specifies one, what needs to be controlled.
>
> **[0:55](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=55)** What needs to be controlled is called the control subject.
>
> **[0:58](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=58)** Control subjects should be the relevant Y and any key X factors.
>
> **[1:03](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=63)** In our office example, the control subject is temperature.
>
> **[1:07](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=67)** Two, the specification, target, or desired range.
>
> **[1:11](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=71)** We can specify the target as 72 degrees Fahrenheit.
>
> **[1:15](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=75)** And it is too hot when it is above 75 degrees, and too cold when it's below 67 degrees.
>
> **[1:23](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=83)** So the desired range is 67 to 75 degrees.
>
> **[1:28](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=88)** Three, how feedback on actual performance is provided, including how the measurement is made, how frequently, and where it is recorded.
>
> **[1:37](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=97)** In our example, using a room thermometer read by someone assigned to that role, the temperature is to be checked and recorded every hour.
>
> **[1:48](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=108)** Four, when to take action and what action to take.
>
> **[1:52](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=112)** Leave the temperature alone when it is between 67 and 75 degrees.
>
> **[1:58](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=118)** Take action as follows.
>
> **[1:59](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=119)** When the temperature is above 75, turn on the AC.
>
> **[2:04](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=124)** When the temperature is below 67 degrees, turn off the AC and if needed, turn on the heat.
>
> **[2:10](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=130)** Five, who is responsible and authorized to monitor and take action?
>
> **[2:16](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=136)** In our example, we can state that the person assigned is responsible for temperature and is authorized to take action and specify that results and actions taken should be recorded.
>
> **[2:27](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=147)** To summarize, the control plan specifies what needs to be controlled, the specification target or desired range, how feedback on actual performance is provided, when to take action and what action to take, and finally, who is responsible and authorized.
>
> **[2:45](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=165)** With a control plan embedded in the standard procedures and operating instructions, the folks running the process have the ability and the means to control the process so that it performs well day in and day out.
>
> **[2:58](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/six-sigma-foundations/the-control-plan-2?u=76281980&t=178)** Now that we have a control plan down, nobody in your office should ever shiver or sweat again.
