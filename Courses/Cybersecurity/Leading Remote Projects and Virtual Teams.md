> [!transcript]- Transcript
>
> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/leading-remote-projects-and-virtual-teams/communicating-with-project-team-members-virtually?u=76281980&t=0)** - Communicating with your team members and other stakeholders, is critical to the success of all projects and even more so when people are working virtually. You have lots of options for how you communicate so let's talk about how to choose the right medium for the message. First, you need to know communication is situational. There's a great media richness model that can guide you to select the right medium for your message. Basically, medium richness means the ability of a medium, such as video conferencing or email, to communicate and change understanding. Communication mediums can be placed on a scale of rich to lean. Rich mediums mean that more information is communicated, lean mediums allow less. Let's look at two ends of the spectrum. Face to face is the richest medium because people can absorb multiple information cues, such as body language, tone of voice, gestures, sentiment and facial expression. Obviously, we don't have that option with [Virtual Teams](../../Skills/Cybersecurity/Virtual%20Teams.md). As you can see on the graphic, with a virtual team, the richest medium we have is video conferencing. Texting is on the other end of the spectrum. Texting isn't a great medium for communicating anything except the most basic information. To take advantage of this wide range of options, we need to know what medium to use when.
>
> **[1:35](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/leading-remote-projects-and-virtual-teams/communicating-with-project-team-members-virtually?u=76281980&t=95)** One of the deciding factors is determining how likely a message is to be misinterpreted or misunderstood. Messages that can be misinterpreted, require richer mediums, such as video conferencing. Messages that are simple can use mediums on the lower end of the scale. The nature of communication also influences the medium. Chatting is fine to respond to, say, simple information, but it's not so good to solve problems or resolve conflict. So for things like problem solving, you need to use the richest form available. Since we're talking about remote projects, that means video conferencing if possible. You should also consider the relationship if you want to grow, nurture, or maintain a relationship, rich mediums are better than lean ones. So how can you apply this information? I'll leave you with some recommendations. Use the richest medium possible for project kickoffs, team meetings, complex conversations, problem solving, project planning and performance feedback. Somewhere in the mid range for exchanging information, dialog and discussion. Use text or chats for very brief conversations or to deliver simple information. The next time you communicate with your team members, think about the type of information, the complexity, the sensitivity of the information
