> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/static-application-security-testing/the-owasp-top-10?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Narrator] [[OWASP]] is one of the most prolific influential organizations, when it comes to securing web applications. The acronym stands for Open [[Web Application Security]] Project. The OWASP Foundation is a not-for-profit organization dedicated to helping developers and security professionals around the world better understand how to secure applications. In the two decades that OWASP has been around, the organization has published a staggering number of [[Application Security]] resources. OWASP groups related resources in projects and they have three distinct project categories. Flagship projects are the most mature, most strategic resources that OWASP has to offer. Production projects is a relatively new project level. This designation is reserved for production ready projects, although the list is still being developed. Other projects are useful, but they're not quite ready for primetime. This list includes projects that were formerly labeled as lab or incubator projects. They include specific tools or documentation resources that have found a large population of interested users. They also include playground projects where forward thinkers can test out new ideas and hopefully produce something that will ultimately be promoted to either production or flagship status. The most well known flagship project from OWASP
>
> **[1:34](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/static-application-security-testing/the-owasp-top-10?u=76281980&t=94)** is the Top 10 Project. The Top 10 represents a rotating list of the most critical web application security risks. OWASP published the very first Top 10 list back in 2003. The following year, they locked in an official version controlled list that they committed to updating every three years going forward.
