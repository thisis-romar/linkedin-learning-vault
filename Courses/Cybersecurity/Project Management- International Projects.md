> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/project-management-international-projects/avoiding-the-illusion?u=76281980&t=0)** - The famous Irish playwright George Bernard Shaw is quoted as saying, "The single biggest problem with communication "is the illusion that it has taken place." This is what happened in one of my projects where people thought they had communicated, but actually it was just the illusion they had, which dealt a costly setback. During a conference call between a team in the U.S., Germany, and China, a request was made for the team in China to make five prototypes based on a drawing. Since the parts were fragile, they were to be packed in a special, padded box and shipped to the U.S. The project leader in Germany asked, "Are all the details clear?" Three weeks later, the parts arrived in an envelope, not a special box; and when the team in the U.S. opened it, the parts inside were broken. After doing some root cause analysis, we realized not everyone had been on the same page. The problem happened because of the illusion Mr. Shaw talked about. So it is communication, and how can we avoid problems caused by this illusion. First, communication comes from the same source as the [word](../../Skills/Cloud%20Computing/Microsoft%20Word.md) common, which implies two people will have the same understanding for something. They'll share a common meaning. Anything outside of this is as Shaw stated: an illusion that can negatively impact the outcome of your tasks. > **[1:36](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/project-management-international-projects/avoiding-the-illusion?u=76281980&t=96)** "They received their instructions." Regardless of all the technology out there, we still depend on humans to reach a common understanding, and by acting as customers and suppliers with responsibility to each other, we can avoid the illusion that communication happened,

> **[3:11](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/project-management-international-projects/avoiding-the-illusion?u=76281980&t=191)** and our project will be more likely to succeed.
