> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/it-service-desk-careers-and-certifications/it-service-desk-responsibilities?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Instructor] Within all IT departments, the IT service desk is a core service. The IT service desk is the first point of contact for end users when they want to report an incident, have a request for something new, and any other IT-related query. There are three key functions of the IT service desk: incident management, request for service, and communication. So let's break those three areas into more detail. The IT service desk owns the incident management process for the IT department. That ranges from incident logging and categorization, incident management support, incident resolution, and incident reporting. A request for service is a new request, it isn't a fault. The end user needs something new. This could be a request for [[Hardware]] or software or access to files or folders. The IT service desk will log and categorize these requests and, where possible, action them. The IT service desk acts as a single point of contact to and from the end users within the business. The IT service desk will be the first point of contact for the end users and the primary contact that they will have with the IT department. But it's not only incoming communication the IT service desk is in charge of. They are also in charge of outgoing communications, notifying the end users of outages, planned works, and any other changes. A lot of businesses set up their IT service desk based on the Information Technology Infrastructure Library,
>
> **[1:34](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/it-service-desk-careers-and-certifications/it-service-desk-responsibilities?u=76281980&t=94)** or [[ITIL]] framework. The ITIL framework offers businesses guidance and the best practices for operating an IT service desk and other IT department processes. The framework is constantly updated to align with the latest IT trends and changes. It is an internationally recognized framework and is a common language that IT service providers and departments use. Each business may set up the IT service desk slightly differently based on its needs, size, and staff skill, but you should find that incident management, request for service, and communication are the key focus areas for the IT service desks.

> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/it-service-desk-careers-and-certifications/project-management-certifications?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Instructor] Project management is the process of leading a team to achieve a goal within given constraints. As an IT service desk engineer, you won't necessarily be leading projects. Chances are you'll be a resource on projects, but a better understanding of project management and how to effectively use it is a key skill for anyone within the IT department. Project management is a wide area, and there are different project management methodologies. Organizations can use multiple project management methodologies or focus on one. Unfortunately, there is no one universal way to manage projects. I want to focus on three project management certifications I have seen peers and colleagues study for and gain from. The first one is PRINCE2 Project Management Certification. PRINCE2 is a process-based project management methodology. There are two levels to this certification, foundation and practitioner. The foundational certification never expires, while the practitioner level expires after five years. After first passing, you can retake the practitioner exam every three years to stay up to date. PRINCE2 is a worldwide recognized certification. I have had technical and non-technical IT colleagues gain either one or both of these certifications. Gaining even the foundational certification will give you additional skills to use in your current role or future, especially if you want to be a team leader or move into project management. The Certified Associate in Project Management, or CAPM, is aimed at project team members
>
> **[1:36](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/it-service-desk-careers-and-certifications/project-management-certifications?u=76281980&t=96)** or new project managers or even students. This qualification is for those looking to demonstrate their project management experience and grow their career. To gain this qualification, you need to meet some prerequisites. Firstly, a secondary degree defined as a high school diploma, GED, associate degree, or the global equivalent, and have at least 23 hours of project management education. There are several in-person or online courses available to fulfill this requirement. The CAPM qualification is great for those just starting with project management. If you have more project management experience, then the Project Management Professional, or PMP Certification, might be more appropriate. The PMP Certification recognizes those that have proven skills in project management. You really should have skills in [[People Management]], understand different project management approaches, and which project benefits from the different approaches, and have a good knowledge of business environments. While this isn't a certification I'd recommend for an IT service desk engineer, it is something a service desk manager might pursue. Having a project management certification is only ever going to benefit you in your career.
