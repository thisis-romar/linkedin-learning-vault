Scope, time and cost. We've been using it for years to measure the success of our projects, but delivering on time, on budget and within scope doesn't mean the customer's going to love what we produce. Projects and programs are successful when we produce solutions our customers find high value while also ensuring what's produced enables our organizations to achieve its strategy. We make this happen through benefits realization. According to the Project Management Institute, benefits realization consists of the gains resulting from the use of outputs created from portfolios, programs and projects. The idea of managing benefits or the practice of benefits realization management is the day-to-day work that is performed to achieve and sustain these benefits. Benefits realization activities ensures the organization receives value from the projects and programs they initiate. When benefits realization practices are in place, the delivery team intentionally focuses on customer value. This is very different to how a lot of teams operate where they focus heavily on the tactical elements of completing deliverables. By focusing only on the deliverables, the team doesn't tie their work to the higher level objectives.
>
> **[1:34](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/what-is-business-benefits-realization?u=76281980&t=94)** Sure, they may complete the project in line with the constraints in the iron triangle, but they miss the big picture of delivering value to the customer. Business benefits realization is important to a lot of people across the organization, from the strategic decision-makers all the way down to the delivery teams who build the solutions that reach the customer. It's a mindset, it's a way of working, and it's an approach, a better approach to how we traditionally fund change initiatives and execute programs and projects. The fundamentals of business benefits realization include; identifying the right initiatives to invest in, identifying expected benefits from those initiatives, building and delivering the solutions that deliver the value, measuring benefits to validate that value was delivered, and sustaining value when the product is released and into the future. Think about sustainment like this. The environment is always changing and there are always internal and external factors that impact the value initially realized. These so-called value blockers, they can be anything from employee turnover, resulting in inefficient use or understanding of a solution to the introduction of new technologies that change user expectations about what's valuable. If internal or external situations are not monitored, a solution delivered at the start of the year that wowed the customer could soon lose its wow factor

Business benefits realization is about making smart choices regarding which portfolios, programs and projects to invest in. Once funded, business benefits realization ensures that the expected benefits are delivered, measured and sustained over time.

#### [Key terms and principles of business benefits realization](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/key-terms-and-principles-of-business-benefits-realization?u=76281980)

> [!transcript]- Transcript
>
> **[0:01](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/key-terms-and-principles-of-business-benefits-realization?u=76281980&t=1)** - To learn how to play a sport better, you take lessons to learn the language of the game and the tips to be successful. The same is true with business benefits realization. You need to be aware of the terminology and the factors to help you succeed. So let's begin by recognizing the difference between benefits and value. In the context of business benefits realization, a benefit is a positive gain a stakeholder receives from the outputs and outcomes produced from my portfolio program or project. Value represents the worth of what is received. To determine value, we consider the benefits received in comparison to the cost of obtaining them. This means that benefits minus costs equal value. A complicating factor when determining value is that benefits can be tangible or intangible. And in business benefits realization, we measure both. A tangible benefit can be concretely measured. This can be a percent increase in market share or a reduction in the number of orders placed. An intangible benefit is subjective, which makes it much harder to measure. Improving good will or industry reputation are examples. Speaking of benefits, let's talk about the concept of disbenefits. Disbenefits are outcomes that negatively impact a stakeholder or stakeholder group. For example, when we implement a new application
>
> **[1:36](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/key-terms-and-principles-of-business-benefits-realization?u=76281980&t=96)** to process claims online, customers benefit. They love the convenience, but automating the initial steps of the process also reduces the company's reliance on the claims processors who have been taking the information by phone. The organization invests in the automation with the objective of reducing head count in the call center. The customer is the benefit recipient of the self-service features. While the claims processors are recipients of the disbenefits of reduced hours or job elimination. Now that we've established our terminology, let's take a look at some critical success factors for business benefits realization. The first is executive sponsorship. To achieve a strong adoption of business benefits realization, there needs to be visible sponsorship from the top. This drives the message of its importance and holds leaders accountable for its success. Then make a commitment to the practices. If you're adopting business benefits realization, expect to commit to make funding decisions based on an honest assessment of business value when analyzing a proposed initiative, if sufficient value can't be identified to outweigh the expected costs, the organization has to be willing to walk away from the idea. Third, embrace collaboration. When defining benefits, collaboration is key. Don't make the mistake of dictating the benefits or you'll
>
> **[3:13](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/key-terms-and-principles-of-business-benefits-realization?u=76281980&t=193)** lose engagement before you even get started. Stakeholders must buy in and take part in the benefits process. They will be more likely to understand how they can contribute to monitoring, measuring, and sustaining the benefits over time if they take part in the process at the onset. Then choose metrics that are unbiased and strong indicators for assessing value. Make sure the organization has the capability to collect their measurements. The data is available at the right intervals and timeframes and is not cost-prohibitive. And finally, continuously improve. When rolling out the practice, don't try to take on so much change that no progress is made at all. Instead start with the basics and then monitor how the rollout of the practice is going. Obtain feedback, document lessons learned, and continuously improve the practice over time.

Together, the vision and mission provides insight as to the purpose of the business and what the business aspires to be in its future. > [!transcript]- Transcript

> **[0:01](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/step-one-identifying-benefits?u=76281980&t=1)** - Would you make a personal investment in something that you knew nothing about? Of course not. And organizations don't either. Instead, organizations instill practices, like the application of business benefits realization to guide their investment decisions. Of course, every organization is unique and will develop these practices differently. But overall, there are some core steps that every business benefits process should cover. As I explain how to perform business benefits realization, I'll refer to the steps collectively as our business benefits realization framework. The framework represents steps from benefits identification through benefit sustainment. Let's begin by taking a look at step one, identify benefits. As evidenced in its name, step one involves the activities to define the benefits that our proposed initiative will deliver. This is a critical step because benefits are used by decision makers when deciding which projects and programs to approve. Organizations with mature business, benefits realization processes approve the projects and programs that have the potential to deliver the most value. If a key benefit is overlooked or misrepresented, the potential value a project or program could provide is misrepresented. The outputs from step one are a benefits register and a business case. The register communicates through grade two list
>
> **[1:35](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/step-one-identifying-benefits?u=76281980&t=95)** of expected benefits. While the business case is the documented feasibility study that includes information to justify the investment in the change initiative. Both outputs can be created at the project or program level, based on where benefit discussions are occurring. The inputs to perform this work consists of the information provided by your business stakeholders when brainstorming the benefits list or discussing the proposed business initiative. To perform step one, you'll have discussions with various stakeholder groups to develop the list of expected benefits. #### [Creating a business case](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/creating-a-business-case?u=76281980)

> [!transcript]- Transcript
>
> **[0:01](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/creating-a-business-case?u=76281980&t=1)** - Deciding to buy a new car is a pretty big deal for most people. And how one arrives at this decision may vary. But what doesn't vary is that the car shopper will have a need. They will have a list of requirements they are looking to satisfy and they'll perform some form of analysis to help them make the decision about which car to buy. This is exactly how organizations make decisions to when deciding whether to fund a new idea, businesses will define the need, identify the requirements, and perform the research to help make the decision. The document that supports this work is the business case. The business case is used to pitch the idea in order to secure funding. And it includes a feasibility study. The study is undertaken to determine whether it's feasible for the business to spend money on addressing a certain situation or not. The situation can be a business problem they want to address, or it can be an opportunity they'd like to pursue. For example, a call center manager recognizes their existing call center software is not capable of supporting future growth. The technology is outdated and lacking in capability, and the manager wants to consider a replacement system to better address their needs. A business case is started. The situation is explained. Some information on the current state is provided for context and an explanation of an envisioned future state is defined.
>
> **[1:35](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/creating-a-business-case?u=76281980&t=95)** Clear measurable objectives are included providing a way to measure success. Should this initiative be pursued since there are different ways to solve the business problem, research is performed to determine different solution options. And a number of ideas are considered and analyzed. Some ideas may fall off the list after research has completed while other options look promising and they remain. Each viable option is reviewed from a cost benefit perspective. Risk, constraints, assumptions, and dependencies are noted, and the results are pulled together in the business case. Once completed the business case is distributed to the decision makers who will use this research to determine whether the funding of this initiative makes sense. There are a few things that need to be part of every business case. First always include at least three viable solutions. As the author of the business case, you need to demonstrate that you have conducted enough analysis to fairly consider different solutions for resolving the situation at hand and provide viable options, avoid putting in non-viable options as that will only skew objectivity. Second, present the information in a non-biased way. While there may be a clear solution in your mind, every option should be given equal consideration. And third include a do nothing option. As odd as this might sound,
>
> **[3:07](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/business-benefits-realization-foundations/creating-a-business-case?u=76281980&t=187)** decision makers always have the option to do nothing, and they will often ask what happens if I choose not to act. Business cases are high value deliverables as organizations who build them, make funding decisions based on research and understanding how investments will realize their strategy.
