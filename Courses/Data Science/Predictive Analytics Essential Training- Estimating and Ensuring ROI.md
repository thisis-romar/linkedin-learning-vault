#### [Why estimating ROI is perceived to be challenging](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/predictive-analytics-essential-training-estimating-and-ensuring-roi/why-estimating-roi-is-perceived-to-be-challenging?u=76281980)

>
> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/predictive-analytics-essential-training-estimating-and-ensuring-roi/why-estimating-roi-is-perceived-to-be-challenging?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Instructor] Many analysts who I speak to about ROI and [[Predictive Analytics]] suggest that it's a fool's errand. As I make their case, you might agree that they have a point. It seems to make sense, but let me make the counterargument. You might be tempted by them, even in instances where they are not the best choice. The trick is to focus on how the model is going to be used. One of my favorite thought leaders in analytics is Tom Khabaza. Tom's value law states, "The value of [[Data Mining]] results is not determined by the accuracy or stability of predictive models.
