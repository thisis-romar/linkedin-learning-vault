CRISP-DM stands for cross-industry standard process for [[Data Mining]]. It's also business-first, ensuring projects solve real-world problems rather than just building something technically impressive. And it's widely applicable, working across structured data, unstructured text, and machine learning models. Whether you've used CRISP-DM before or not, its phases map closely to the real-data science work, making it the perfect blueprint for this course where we will explore how [[Generative AI|GenAI]] enhances each step. Now let's recap its key phases. CRISP-DM consists of six core phases in total. First, there's business understanding, the foundation of any successful data science project. Before diving into data, we need to clearly define what we're trying to achieve. That starts with setting business objectives. What are we solving and how will we measure success? Assessing the situation, understanding constraints, available resources, and potential roadblocks.
