At the core of generative models are [Algorithms](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Algorithms.md), specifically, [Neural Networks](../../Skills/Artificial%20Intelligence%20(AI)/Neural%20Networks.md) that are designed to understand data in a way that mimics human cognition. These neural networks are trained on vast datasets, but unlike traditional machine learning models that make predictions based on input data, generative models can create new data that wasn't in their training set. Think of a neural network as a complex web of interconnected nodes each representing a mathematical function. Each node receives inputs, processes them through a series of mathematical operations, and then passes the result to the next layer of nodes. This hierarchical structure allows the network to learn from the data by adjusting the internal parameters, essentially fine-tuning itself to make increasingly accurate predictions or generate more realistic data. In generative models, this network is divided into two main components, the generator and the discriminator. The generator's role is to produce data that resembles the training set, while the discriminator's job is to distinguish between real data from the training set and generated fake data. These two components engage in a continuous loop
>
> **[1:34](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/leveraging-generative-ai-in-finance-and-accounting/understanding-generative-models?u=76281980&t=94)** of feedback and adjustment. Over time, the generator becomes increasingly proficient at creating data so convincing that the discriminator can hardly tell it apart from the real thing. This iterative process, often referred to as a generative adversarial network, or GAN, enables the model to generate new data points that are not only statistically similar to the training set, but also rich in contextual nuances. Now let's break this down a bit. In the realm of FP&A, this means that a generative model can simulate financial scenarios based on historical data and current variables. It can then generate a range of possible financial outcomes from best case to worst case scenarios and everything in between. Imagine a neural network trained not just on generic [Financial Data](../../Skills/Software%20Development/Financial%20Data.md), but on your company's specific financial history, market trends, and even news feeds.
