> [!transcript]- Transcript
>
> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=0)** - There's no guarantees with [[Social Media Marketing]].

> **[0:21](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=21)** Social media measurement is the practice of setting meaningful goals, tracking your progress in achieving those results, and using this performance data to inform your decision making.

>
> **[0:44](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=44)** For instance, growing your audience on social media by 10% in the next 90 days, or doubling the conversion rate of your paid social campaigns by next quarter are both examples of meaningful goals.

> **[1:08](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=68)** Conversion goals like earning sales, or generating leads are the most important type as they're the most valuable actions a person can take when engaging with your social media.
>
> **[1:19](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=79)** The next category are engagement goals, which are less important than conversions, but indicate the amount and quality of interactions your social media campaigns are driving.
>
> **[1:29](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=89)** Like, for example, when a social post earns a significant number of shares and comments, it's an indication that your content was relevant enough to drive conversation and feedback from audiences.

> **[1:58](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=118)** The best case scenario is to monitor a mix of goals, but for some campaigns, one goal will suffice.

> **[2:06](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=126)** As you're deciding what goals to focus on, consider how you'll track your progress in reaching them by choosing complimentary metrics and relevant measurement tools.
>
> **[2:16](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=136)** Metrics are a unit of measure that indicate how far along you are in reaching a specific goal.
>
> **[2:22](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=142)** For example, if the goal is growing your audience on social media by 10% in 90 days, a metric you could look to is the change in the number of followers on each social network.
>
> **[2:33](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=153)** If your goal is improved brand awareness, then metrics like impressions, views and reach would be helpful for monitoring your efforts on TikTok and [[Instagram]] specifically.
>
> **[2:45](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=165)** While there's many to choose from, pick metrics that indicate your progress, ones that are well established in the industry and are straightforward to calculate using the right tools.
>
> **[2:56](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=176)** It's common to rely on a mix of measurement tools.
>
> **[2:59](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=179)** Often this toolkit is comprised of out-of-the-box analytics tools provided by the social networks, in addition to third party tools.

> **[3:14](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=194)** And finally, generate reporting from these tools so you can reference the performance from your recent social media efforts and use it to make more informed marketing decisions.

> **[3:33](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=213)** It's also a good idea to generate reports after each campaign is completed so you can assess how it performed, lessons learned and what to do differently next time.

> **[3:42](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/social-media-marketing-foundations-22309474/focus-on-social-media-measurement?u=76281980&t=222)** Review these reports for elements that may have impacted performance that you could duplicate again, like your coverage of certain topics, use of select content formats, or mentions from prominent voices.
