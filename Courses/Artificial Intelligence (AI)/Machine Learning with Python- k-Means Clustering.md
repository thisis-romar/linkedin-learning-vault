>
> **[0:09](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=9)** These subgroups are known as clusters.

> **[0:11](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=11)** Clustering is an unsupervised machine learning approach.
>
> **[0:15](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=15)** This means that we use clustering when working with previously unlabeled data.
>
> **[0:20](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=20)** There are two primary goals to clustering.
>
> **[0:23](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=23)** The first is to ensure that the items within a particular cluster are as similar as possible.
>
> **[0:29](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=29)** This is known as high-intraclass similarity.
>
> **[0:33](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=33)** The second goal to clustering is to make sure the items within one cluster are as different as possible from items in another cluster.
>
> **[0:42](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=42)** This is known as low-interclass similarity.
>
> **[0:45](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=45)** Clustering is a widely used machine learning approach.
>
> **[0:49](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=49)** It is sometimes used in the domain of network security as a way to detect anomalous behavior in computer networks.
>
> **[0:56](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=56)** Clustering is also sometimes used to automatically group or categorize documents based on similarity.
>
> **[1:04](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=64)** Another common use of clustering is to segment customers for marketing purposes.
>
> **[1:09](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=69)** To illustrate this use case, let's assume that we work for a small credit union and that we have the data shown here for each of our card customers.
>
> **[1:19](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=79)** Our objective with this data could be to group or segment these customers based on spending score and income level.
>
> **[1:28](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=88)** Using a scale of low to high for both variables, we can represent the data this way in 2 dimensional space.
>
> **[1:35](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=95)** A clustering algorithm could then assign each customer to a group based on how similar they are to other customers.
>
> **[1:43](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=103)** In this example, the algorithm assigned each customer to one of three clusters.
>
> **[1:48](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=108)** This means that customers that end up in the same cluster are very similar in terms of their income and spending habits.
>
> **[1:55](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=115)** These clusters have no intrinsic meaning other than that they represent closely related customers.
>
> **[2:02](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=122)** It is up to us to assign contextual labels to each of the clusters.
>
> **[2:07](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=127)** For example, we could decide to assign the labels alpha, beta, and theta, three clusters.
>
> **[2:14](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=134)** By doing this, we are implicitly assigning labels to every item within each cluster.
>
> **[2:21](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=141)** Because of our ability to apply labels to previously unlabel data in this way, clustering is also sometimes referred to as unsupervised classification.
>
> **[2:30](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=150)** There are several approaches to clustering each with its own strengths and weaknesses.
>
> **[2:35](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=155)** The type of clustering one chooses to use is often dependent on the characteristics of the data and the type of clusters needed.
>
> **[2:44](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=164)** Clustering can be hierarchical.
>
> **[2:46](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=166)** With hierarchical clustering, clusters are nested within each other.
>
> **[2:51](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=171)** This means that the boundaries of a particular cluster can fall within the boundaries of another cluster creating a parent/child relationship.
>
> **[3:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=180)** This nested structure between clusters creates a hierarchy that is often represented in the form of a cluster tree known as a dendrogram.
>
> **[3:09](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=189)** Clustering can be partitional.
>
> **[3:12](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=192)** With partitional clustering, each cluster boundary is independent of the others.
>
> **[3:17](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=197)** There is no hierarchy between clusters.
>
> **[3:20](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=200)** Partitional clustering divides data objects into non overlapping groups.
>
> **[3:25](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=205)** In other words, no object can be a member of more than one cluster, and every cluster must have at least one object.
>
> **[3:35](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=215)** Clustering can also be overlapping.
>
> **[3:37](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/machine-learning-with-python-k-means-clustering/what-is-clustering?u=76281980&t=217)** As the name implies an overlapping cluster is one where the boundaries of one cluster can overlap with those of other clusters.
>
