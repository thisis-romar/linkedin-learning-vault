> **[0:00](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/openai-api-speech/text-to-speech-overview?u=76281980&t=0)** - [Instructor] Text-to-speech or speech synthesis has been around for decades now. Up until recently, these systems would have an unauthentic sound and at times, would be quite unclear. This has been changing quite rapidly. New models such as OpenAI's Whisper sounds much clearer when reading texts. Now, where do we see text-to-speech or speech synthesis? Well, accessibility software will often rely on speech synthesis to read out loud things to blind people or people that have a hard time reading. Then there are virtual assistants such as Alexa, Siri, or [[Google]] Assistant that you speak to, and once a command is processed, you get feedback. That feedback uses speech synthesis. Finally, digital media will often use speech synthesis to read articles to people so that you can listen to articles on the go. The better speech synthesis gets, the more use cases will arise. So this is a great time to learn these APIs and to start thinking of innovative ways to incorporate speech synthesis in your software.
