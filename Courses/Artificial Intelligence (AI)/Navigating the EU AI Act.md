The market surveillance authority is responsible for communicating the details of the incident to the national public authorities and the European Commission. The exact information that should be prepared for those groups is still being defined by the commission and will likely become available next May. Following all serious incidents, providers should conduct necessary investigations, including a full risk assessment and determining corrective actions.
>
> **[1:32](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/navigating-the-eu-ai-act/incident-information-sharing?u=76281980&t=92)** The provider must collaborate with the competent authorities and notified bodies during this process. It is absolutely mandatory that nothing be done to alter the AI system that would impact a third-party evaluation of the cause of the incident. This ensures that providers cannot cover up issues caused by their AI system. Again, as much as we plan to implement controls to maintain the health and safety of EU citizens, accidents will happen and people will inevitably be hurt. We must be aware of our response mechanisms and follow them when necessary. If you are a provider or deployer, stay diligent in your monitoring and raise your hand immediately when issues arise. The worst thing you can do is not say anything and hope regulators don't notice.
