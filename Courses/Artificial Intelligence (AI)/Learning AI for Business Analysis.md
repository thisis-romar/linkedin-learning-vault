Outcomes require us to rethink how we define success in our work. We used to be successful in business analysis, creating a requirements document on time, but that doesn't ensure that the right thing was built. So what do we measure? I recommend outcome measurements that measure user behavior. These are human behaviors that leaders and the team are looking to shift with the new technology in order to meet business objectives. For example, we are looking to decrease the amount of time customers and agents spend on a call to resolve a customer issue. Here, the behavior is customers are able to end the call sooner, and the outcome or measurement is decreased time on call, and the business objective this maps to is a lower cost per call and better customer satisfaction. The role of the business analyst in AI

Each item we work on should show progress toward the tracked metric and outcome. We track the measurement often and ongoing to see if the right work is being done and is moving the metric in the right direction rather than waiting to the end of the entire scope being complete to measure outcomes. Well, when we use an outcome measurement to guide our approach with small pieces of work and we use experiments and hypotheses to learn, we are poised for success with AI.
